## Introduction

## Before the Year Starts

#### Math Preparation
Enroll in the math camp course. At UCI, this was a short two-week course with a take-home midterm and a final. I didn't really understand a lot of material from the math camp, but it can be useful in terms of settling in and making friends.

#### Coding and Workflow
I learnt some MATLAB in math camp, but I really didn't get a lot out of it. You don't need any additional preparation for coding (especially where you can learn and drive a lot with AI). Maybe spend some time preparing folders and setting up a calendar with holidays and important dates you may need to remember. I also spent some time setting up a Notion page and writing goals.

#### Logistics
Take the summer before grad school to honestly enjoy. See friends and family. Travel. Just sit down and do nothing, because such time will be hard to come by.

## Coursework

At UCI, we had a quarter system so basically took 3 sequences for each course. 

#### Microeconomics
- Micro 1: Producer and consumer theory, risk and uncertainity, competitive equilibria, externalities
- Micro 2: Game theory
- Micro 3: Mechanism design
Personally, my ranking for difficulty of content goes as Micro 1 < Micro 3 < Micro 2. I have always sort of struggled with Game Theory topics.

#### Macroeconomics
- Macro 1: Growth models, optimal control, dynamic programming
- Macro 2: Rational expectations, complete markets, real business cycle, New Keynesian
- Macro 3: Incomplete markets (+ Matlab implementations of such)
Macro 1 is the hardest because the professor designs it as such. I got my lowest total aggregate percentage for that class. Macro 2 was easier as it was pretty computational and there was a clear way to solve every problem. Macro 3 took a lot of work because of the coding aspect of the course but the theory was easier to digest.

#### Econometrics
- Metrics 1: Frequentist 
- Metrics 2: Additional topics: focus on Bayesian methods
- Metrics 3: Additional topics: focus on Bayesian methods
I didn't really pay attention to any of econometrics because UCI didn't have qualifying exams for this. Metrics 1 required work because it had both a midterm and a final, but for 2 & 3 we had the same professor, and we convinced him for both quarters to not give us an exam. The HWs were coding heavy but all doable.
  
## Research and Seminars

#### Finding Research Direction
I came in with wanting to do applied micro. Studying more macro only cemented my desire for the former. I just hated the way macro was structured at UCI. Macro 1 was unbeatably hard - I probably never got a whole question correct for this class ever. Macro 2 was just too much material and nothing really excited me. Macro 3 was a lot of work but it ended up being just fine. It was probably the most exciting class I had among my Macro courses.

#### Attending Seminars
Don't. You are actively discouraged from attending them since your time is very limited and it is better utilized studying or doing anything else. Go if there's free food or it's extra credit, or if there's a piece of research or a person you're genuinely interested to interact with.

#### Talking to ~Faculty~ TAs and Upperclassmen
I didn't care too much in talking to faculty. TAs were the most helpful in talking through both class requirements and qual preparation. We were really lucky to get a great bunch of TAs across the board.

## Routines

#### Studying
I structured my studying specifically for every professor. For classes, you're basically trying to understand what the professor focuses on and likes to test the students on. You're obviously trying to build a solid foundation for everything in your first year but ultimately you might only end up using a fraction of that knowledge in your research. My goal for first year was just to pass classes, have a good GPA (it doesn't matter according to people, but it did for me personally), and pass my quals in my first try.
My approach for quarters 1 and 2 was to basically study for the classes and pass. I did not have any study plan for the quals. I did study macro 2 and micro 1 for the quals as I was taking those classes. I had somehow given up on macro 3 

#### Rest
Got the worst possible sleep in Q3. I honestly hated up waking up from my sleep because it implied my rest time was over. I slept less and remember sleeping 4-5 hours during my last weeks leading up to the qual. I restricted my social media time aggressively: to like 30 mins to an hour everyday, which is a lot for someone who was chronically online. I had a pretty good working out schedule for the first two quarters but had to also give that up as I got to my third quarter. I also didn't follow any series and just spent as much as time I could to get rid of distractions. It worked just okay - I did have days where I just rested and didn't do any work outside of classes. In Q1 and Q2, I barely did any work on Fridays. I am hoping for a more structured future years as I don't have to study as hard, and research is hopefully more self-driven.

#### Community
This is what got me through the whole of the year. My cohort! We were a lot of people: 17 overall, but I found a cool smaller group that hung out more consistently and was always available to help. We used to go to a bar after every A great group of women! We did girls nights.
